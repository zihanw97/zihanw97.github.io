Ph.D. Openings | 2026/2027

Join the AIM² Lab

Fully funded Ph.D. positions in AI for materials, engineering design, and scientific machine learning at Oakland University.

Doctoral Study

Fully funded Ph.D. positions

The AIM² Lab is recruiting Ph.D. students for 2026/2027 admission. Admitted students will receive full financial support, including tuition and a living stipend, along with computing resources, research mentorship, publication support, and opportunities to attend conferences and participate in collaborative projects.

Research Areas

What you could work on

  • AI-driven materials discovery and engineering design
  • Generative design, graph neural networks, and deep generative models
  • Bayesian optimization, active learning, and autonomous experiment or simulation design
  • Uncertainty quantification, robust design, and reliability analysis
  • Physics-informed machine learning and simulation-experiment data fusion

Candidate Profile

Who should apply

We welcome applicants from mechanical engineering, materials science, computer science, physics, applied mathematics, and related fields.

  • Experience with Python, PyTorch, or another machine learning framework
  • Background in machine learning, optimization, simulation, scientific computing, or materials and structural design
  • Research projects, publications, competitions, or engineering practice
  • Curiosity, initiative, and a collaborative approach to research

Other Opportunities

Students and collaborators

Motivated undergraduate researchers are welcome to ask about computational and experimental projects. We also welcome collaborations in autonomous experimentation, materials discovery, robust design, digital twins, additive manufacturing, and metamaterial systems.

How to Apply

Contact the AIM² Lab

Prospective Ph.D. students should email a short introduction, research interests, CV, transcript, and links to relevant projects or publications. Please briefly explain how your background and goals connect with the AIM² Lab's research directions.
