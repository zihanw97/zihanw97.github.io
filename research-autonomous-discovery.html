Research Direction 01

Autonomous design and discovery

AI agents that learn from simulation, experiments, and one another to accelerate scientific discovery.

← All research directions

The Challenge

Discovery workflows remain fragmented

Conventional research often separates modeling, experimentation, and decision-making into slow sequential steps. This limits exploration of high-dimensional material and design spaces and delays learning from new evidence.

Our Approach

Learn continuously across digital and physical evidence

We develop closed-loop systems that connect high-throughput simulation, digital twins, physical experiments, literature, and adaptive learning. A reasoning engine coordinates specialized agents while decentralized optimization enables them to exchange information and explore collaboratively.

Collaborative optimization

Resource-aware Bayesian optimization coordinates heterogeneous agents, tasks, budgets, and design spaces.

Multi-fidelity learning

Models combine evidence at different costs and resolutions to decide where the next evaluation is most valuable.

Scientific AI agents

LLM-guided agents support hypothesis generation, knowledge transfer, experiment planning, and interpretable decisions.

Research Goal

Toward self-driving discovery laboratories

These methods aim to make discovery faster, more adaptive, and more data-efficient while preserving physical insight and experimental accountability.
