Research Direction 03

Uncertainty-aware reliable design

Quantifying what models know, what they do not know, and how variability affects engineering decisions.

← All research directions

The Challenge

Point predictions can conceal risk

Material data are often sparse, noisy, and heterogeneous. Models that report a single answer without calibrated confidence can fail silently when conditions shift, creating uncertainty in performance and reliability.

Our Approach

Make uncertainty part of the design loop

We develop statistical learning and physics-informed methods that quantify and propagate both aleatoric uncertainty from data and epistemic uncertainty from models. This evidence guides robust optimization, targeted data acquisition, and adaptive model refinement.

Calibrated uncertainty

Probabilistic models provide confidence estimates that can be evaluated and propagated across design scales.

Out-of-distribution detection

Models identify unfamiliar conditions and signal when predictions require new evidence or higher-fidelity analysis.

Risk-aware optimization

Active learning and robust design select informative data and solutions that remain reliable under variability.

Research Goal

Trustworthy predictions and robust decisions

These methods support repeatable performance, transparent model use, and better decisions for complex materials and mechanical systems.
